Home

Team: Elizabeth Codick, Mark Thurrott, Chad Cummings

Customer:

  • Video gamers who are more of visualatory than auditory
  • deaf/HoH gamers
  • Gamers who experiences hearing-loss

The problem:

  • video games are lacking visualization noises (or any form of accessibility) for deaf/HoH video gamers
  • The video games industry and culture are on the rise, but people seems fail/forget to follow up with accessibility in video games, especially competitive or casual multiplayer video games where players are playing in real time action.

An analysis of the problem:

  • adding some kind of icons or aid pointing the deaf/HoH gamers where the noises are coming from
  • Adding an overlay or UI element in-game that show deaf/HoH gamers where the sounds/noises are coming from as if deaf/HoH gamers could “hear” prior making an action decision

Pain points:

  • Unequal access to information (sound location or description) given by the game.
  • Unable to fill the skill gap between deaf/HoH gamers and hearing gamers because of hearing abilities
  • Getting killed from behind

Covenants:

  • We will...
    • Discuss ideas openly and freely (there are no bad ideas)
    • Allow time to meet in person or discuss online tasks to be completed
    • Complete designated tasks
    • If unsure ask questions or ask for help

Stakeholders

Goals:

  • Designing a concept for accessible video games. Creating an app to accommodate for deaf/HoH
  • Close the skill gap between deaf/HoH and hearing gamers
  • Give the deaf/HoH gamers a chance to play video games in the competitive level without being burden
  • Aid the deaf/Hoh gamers without giving them an advantage over video gamers who don’t have the aid

Worries:

  • Hearing users may be annoyed with the accessibility features
  • Other game companies may be against this feature
  • Others may consider this aid as cheating the system/game

Ideas:

  • Provide an option for the accessibility features
  • Provide an ability to “whitelist” the certain sounds (e.g., capturing the sounds of players walking/running footsteps and guns shooting) so we know where the noises are coming from AND what noises were made

How to involve them:

  • Get feedback an different steps of the progress
    • Design
    • Content
    • Functionality

How to communicate progress:

  • We plan to update the website with the progress of our app on a daily basis.

How to understand their way of communicating:

  • Understanding the community involved (gamers)
  • Meet to address the ideas of the product and get an idea of what the stakeholders expect

How to communicate the design to them:

  • Make sure to make the stages presented to them are self explanatory
